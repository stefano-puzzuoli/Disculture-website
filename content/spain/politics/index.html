Politics & Economy

Politics: Government

Spain has been a democracy since 1977 when it elected its own government and has been a prosperous democracy since even though it is not a Republic. The offical head of state is King Felipe VI who succeeded his father in June 2014. Under the Spanish constituation the King still hold considerale power as chief executive and commander-in-chief but King Felipe pledged to continue his father's legacy of supporting parliament. The current Prime Minister of Spain is Mariano Rajoy, who was elected in October 2016.

Economy: Industry, Agriculture and Tourism

Spain joined the EU in 1986 and developed a dynamic and rapidly growing economy. The most important sectors being wholesale and retail trade, transport,accoommodation and food services which account for 24.5% of the Spanish economy. Spain's main export partners are France, Germany and the United Kingdom, while it's main import partners are China, Germany and France. Spain faced a prolonged recession in the wake of the global financial crisis that began in 2008 but began to grow again economically in 2014 and has been growing ever since.
